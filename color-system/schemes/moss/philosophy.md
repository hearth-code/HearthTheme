# Moss Philosophy

Moss is a textured material color philosophy for code interfaces.

It still treats color as a semantic language before it becomes a platform theme,
but the goal is no longer "higher energy than Ember".
The goal is to create a distinctly different material world:

- charcoal substrate with a warm bias
- parchment carriers instead of bright paper ink
- pigmented syntax lanes with visible texture and spacing
- the same level of design discipline

## Core Thesis

Moss should feel:

- textured but not noisy
- structured but not clinical
- colorful but not crowded
- tactile but still daily-drivable

If Ember feels like hearthlight on paper, Moss should feel like carbon, vellum, oxidized metal,
and hand-mixed pigments arranged with editorial control.

## Vocabulary

The palette should feel like one material world with several clearly separated syntax lanes.

It speaks through:

- charcoal with a brown bias for substrate
- dry parchment neutrals for readable carriers
- ember and clay heat for control and directives
- lichen and sage for callable structure
- oxidized teal for type structure
- brass, mulberry, and parchment support pigments
- warm gray comments that never become sludge

## Role Philosophy

Colors are assigned by meaning before widget.

- `keyword` and `control` should carry an ember lane that feels designed, not like generic alert red
- `function` should feel lichen-led rather than blue-led, but calmer and drier than a stock green theme
- `type` should carry its own oxidized teal lane rather than dissolve into general warmth
- `string` should feel like brass or lacquered metal, not like a borrowed yellow preset
- `property` should stay near parchment and preserve page rhythm instead of joining the hero color lanes
- `operator`, `variable`, and `parameter` should remain quiet enough to preserve page rhythm
- `comment` should hold the page together through warm gray restraint

This flavor wins through material coherence and lane separation, not through raw saturation.

## Surface Philosophy

Surfaces should carry personality this time.

Unlike Ember, this product should not keep all identity off the substrate.
The background, shells, and paper tones should establish the textural atmosphere immediately.

But the substrate should not do all the work.
If the whole editor becomes uniformly brown, the syntax loses articulation.

The page should therefore feel:

- charcoal first
- warm second
- textured immediately
- never sepia-washed

## Variant Philosophy

The four variants should share one personality:

- `dark`: strongest material identity, clearest syntax lane split, closest to the flagship target
- `darkSoft`: same pigments, lower pressure, no flattening into gray or brown mush
- `light`: textured paper daylight, not modern office cream
- `lightSoft`: quieter and softer, but not washed out or generic

## Product Principle

Moss should be a real second product choice, not an alternate material mood.

It should be the line users choose when they want:

- obvious material texture
- charcoal-and-paper substrate
- stronger role lanes than Ember
- a palette that feels system-native rather than referential

The difference from Ember should be visible immediately, and it should feel like a genuine second taste, not a colder or louder Ember.
